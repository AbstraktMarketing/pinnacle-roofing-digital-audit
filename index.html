What This Means

Pinnacle's rep confirmed paid lead gen — not organic — is currently where the business makes and spends the most money. But both active channels are rented: Google Local Service Ads charges per lead with no keyword-level control, and HomeAdvisor sends the same lead to 3–4 competing contractors simultaneously. Neither builds an owned audience, supports retargeting, or gets more efficient over time. The budget freed up by pausing standard Google Ads is also relevant — it confirms there's already real paid budget in motion, not a new line item to justify from scratch.

The Opportunity
Zero competitors in this tracked set run traditional Google Search Ads. That's not a sign the channel doesn't work — it means nobody is bidding up CPCs on terms like “commercial roofing contractors ct” ($27.50 CPC) or “commercial roofing connecticut” ($61.43 CPC). A well-run Search campaign would own the lead outright instead of sharing it, and could target the exact commercial buyer-intent terms this audit already identified as missing from the organic site.
